## Actions

### 1. Post-Submission Designer Transparency View

**Problem (Audit Rec #7):** After submitting a blueprint for review, the Architect effectively went dark. The pipeline board showed lifecycle status but not the internal narrative — who is reviewing, what step they're on, how long it's been at each step, whether any comments exist.

**Implementation:** Added approval progress timeline to the Blueprint Workbench right rail for blueprints in `in_review` status. The timeline fetches `approvalProgress` from the existing blueprint endpoint (which already returns `ApprovalStepRecord[]`). Each step renders: step number, role label, reviewer name, decision badge (pending / approved / changes-requested / rejected), timestamp, and comment text if present. The display is conditional — only appears when `submitted === true || blueprintStatus === "in_review"`.

**Files modified:** `src/app/blueprints/[id]/page.tsx`

### 2. Pause/Resume Intelligence (Journey 9)

**Problem (Audit Rec #10):** Resuming a draft blueprint meant navigating to the workbench and reconstructing context mentally — how many sections, what violations exist, how many refinements have been made. No "welcome back" synthesis existed.

**Implementation:** Added a `resumeBannerDismissed` state and a staleness check on `updatedAt`. When a blueprint's status is `"draft"` and `updatedAt` is more than 24 hours ago, a contextual banner renders at the top of the workbench: "👋 Welcome back — last edited X days ago" with inline stats (section count, governance violation count, refinement count). A dismiss button hides the banner for the session. This gives the Architect an immediate orientation without requiring them to scroll through the full blueprint.

**Files modified:** `src/app/blueprints/[id]/page.tsx`
